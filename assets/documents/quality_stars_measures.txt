OVERVIEW: CMS STARS RATING PROGRAM
-------------------------------------
The Centers for Medicare & Medicaid Services (CMS) Stars Rating Program evaluates health plans
on a scale of 1 to 5 stars across clinical quality, member experience, and administrative
effectiveness. For commercial plans, analogous quality frameworks (HEDIS, NCQA) use similar
measure sets to assess plan performance.

Key performance thresholds:
  - 4-Star rating: Generally requires performance at or above the 70th–75th percentile nationally
  - 5-Star rating: Requires performance at or above the 90th percentile nationally
  - Impact: Plans achieving 4+ stars qualify for quality bonus payments and higher CMS rebates.
    5-star plans receive the maximum bonus and may market to members year-round. Plans below
    3 stars for 3 consecutive years face potential contract termination.

Care gap closure programs are the primary lever for improving Stars performance. Members who are
eligible for a measure but have not yet received the qualifying service represent an actionable
care gap. Outreach and care management interventions targeting these members simultaneously
improve the plan's Stars score and deliver meaningful clinical benefit to members.


MEASURE: COLORECTAL CANCER SCREENING (COL-E)
----------------------------------------------
Description:    Percentage of members age 45–75 who are continuously enrolled and have received
                appropriate colorectal cancer screening.

Eligible Population:
  - Members age 45–75 as of the end of the measurement year
  - Continuously enrolled in the health plan for the measurement year
  - Exclusions: members with a history of colorectal cancer or total colectomy

Numerator — Member is compliant if ANY of the following are documented:
  - Colonoscopy within the last 10 years
  - Flexible sigmoidoscopy within the last 5 years
  - Annual fecal occult blood test (FOBT) or fecal immunochemical test (FIT) in the measurement year
  - Multi-target stool DNA (Cologuard) within the last 3 years
  - CT colonography within the last 5 years

Care Gap Definition:
  Members who are eligible but have NO qualifying colorectal cancer screening service on record
  within the applicable lookback window represent an open care gap. These members should be
  prioritized for outreach and their PCP notified to facilitate scheduling.

Current Plan Performance Benchmark (4-Star threshold): 72% compliance


MEASURE: DIABETES CARE — EYE EXAM (EED)
-----------------------------------------
Description:    Percentage of members with diabetes (Type 1 or Type 2) age 18–75 who received
                a retinal or dilated eye exam during the measurement year.

Eligible Population:
  - Members with a diagnosis of diabetes (ICD-10: E10.x, E11.x)
  - Age 18–75 as of the end of the measurement year
  - Continuously enrolled for at least 11 of 12 months

Numerator:
  - Documented retinal exam or dilated eye exam by an ophthalmologist or optometrist
  - Reading of a retinal photograph (teleretinal screening) also qualifies
  - Exam must occur within the measurement year (January 1 – December 31)

4-Star Benchmark:    79% of eligible diabetic members with a documented eye exam
Current Gap:         Members with diabetes who have not had an eye exam in over 12 months


MEASURE: DIABETES CARE — HbA1c POOR CONTROL (HBD)
----------------------------------------------------
Description:    Percentage of members with diabetes age 18–75 whose most recent HbA1c result
                is greater than 9.0% (poor control). This is a NEGATIVE measure — lower
                percentage is better.

Eligible Population:
  - Members with Type 1 or Type 2 diabetes, age 18–75
  - At least one HbA1c lab result in the measurement year required for inclusion

Numerator (Negative):
  - Most recent HbA1c value in the measurement year is >9.0%

4-Star Benchmark:    Fewer than 21% of eligible diabetic members with HbA1c >9.0%
Interpretation:      A result below 21% means the plan is performing well on glycemic control.
                     Members with HbA1c >9% represent both a quality gap and a clinical risk
                     requiring care management intervention.


MEASURE: MEDICATION ADHERENCE — DIABETES (MAD)
------------------------------------------------
Description:    Percentage of members on oral diabetes medications with a proportion of days
                covered (PDC) of 80% or greater during the measurement year.

Eligible Population:
  - Members age 18–75 with ≥2 fills of an oral diabetes medication (metformin, sulfonylureas,
    DPP-4 inhibitors, SGLT-2 inhibitors, or thiazolidinediones) during the measurement year

Numerator:
  - Member has PDC ≥80% for their index medication class across the measurement year

PDC Calculation:
  PDC = (Days supply of medication on hand across measurement period) ÷ (Days in measurement period)
  PDC ≥0.80 = adherent

4-Star Benchmark:    87% of eligible members with PDC ≥80%
Outreach Note:       Members with PDC between 60–79% are the highest-yield targets for
                     adherence outreach — they are close to the threshold and often have
                     addressable barriers (cost, side effects, complexity).


MEASURE: MEDICATION ADHERENCE — HYPERTENSION (MAH)
----------------------------------------------------
Description:    Percentage of members on RAS antagonists (ACE inhibitors or ARBs) with PDC ≥80%.

Eligible Population:
  - Members age 18–85 with ≥2 fills of an ACE inhibitor or ARB during the measurement year
  - Diagnosis of hypertension (ICD-10: I10) required

Numerator:
  - PDC ≥80% for ACE inhibitor or ARB class across the measurement year

4-Star Benchmark:    87% of eligible members adherent (PDC ≥80%)


MEASURE: BREAST CANCER SCREENING (BCS)
----------------------------------------
Description:    Percentage of women age 50–74 who had a mammogram in the last 2 years.

Eligible Population:
  - Women age 50–74 as of the end of the measurement year
  - Continuously enrolled; no history of bilateral mastectomy

Numerator:
  - At least one mammogram (screening or diagnostic) on record within the past 2 years

4-Star Benchmark:    78% of eligible women with a mammogram in the last 2 years


MEASURE: STATIN USE IN PERSONS WITH CARDIOVASCULAR DISEASE (SPC)
------------------------------------------------------------------
Description:    Percentage of members age 21–75 with cardiovascular disease (CVD) who are
                currently on statin therapy.

Eligible Population:
  - Members with a diagnosis of IVD (ischemic vascular disease), AMI, CABG, PCI, or peripheral
    vascular disease
  - Age 21–75; continuously enrolled

Numerator:
  - At least one statin prescription fill during the measurement year

4-Star Benchmark:    88% of eligible CVD members on statin therapy
Note:                This measure focuses on treatment initiation and continuation rather than
                     adherence. Members with CVD and no statin fill represent a prescribing gap
                     requiring provider outreach, not just member outreach.


CARE GAP IDENTIFICATION AND OUTREACH
--------------------------------------
Members who are eligible for any of the above measures but have not yet received the qualifying
service represent actionable care gaps. Health plans use claims data, lab results, and pharmacy
data to identify these gaps on a rolling basis.

Best practices for care gap closure:
  - Prioritize members with multiple simultaneous open gaps (highest impact per outreach contact)
  - Leverage predictive risk scores to focus clinical resources on members most likely to respond
  - Coordinate outreach with the member's attributed PCP to reinforce clinical messaging
  - Track gap closure rates by cohort (age band, chronic condition, geography) to identify
    populations with structural barriers to care access

Closing care gaps improves the plan's Stars score and — more importantly — ensures that members
with preventable and manageable conditions receive timely, evidence-based care.
