DRUG TIER STRUCTURE
-------------------
The plan formulary organizes covered medications into five tiers. Member cost-sharing is determined
by the tier assigned to each medication at the time of dispensing.

  Tier 1 — Preferred Generic:       $10 copay per 30-day supply
  Tier 2 — Non-Preferred Generic:   $25 copay per 30-day supply
  Tier 3 — Preferred Brand:         $50 copay per 30-day supply
  Tier 4 — Non-Preferred Brand:     $90 copay per 30-day supply
  Tier 5 — Specialty Medications:   25% coinsurance, not to exceed $250 per 30-day supply

Tier placement is reviewed annually. Members may request a formulary exception if a clinically
necessary medication is not on formulary or is placed at a higher tier than expected.


PRIOR AUTHORIZATION (PA) REQUIREMENTS
--------------------------------------
Prior Authorization is required before the plan will cover certain medications. PA ensures that
medications are used appropriately and that clinically appropriate alternatives have been considered.

Drug classes requiring Prior Authorization include:
  - GLP-1 receptor agonists (e.g., semaglutide, tirzepatide, liraglutide)
  - Specialty biologics (e.g., TNF inhibitors, IL-17 inhibitors, PCSK9 inhibitors)
  - Brand-name medications when a therapeutically equivalent generic is available
  - Opioid analgesics prescribed for more than 7 consecutive days
  - Certain oncology oral medications
  - Human growth hormone and related peptides

PA requests must be submitted by the prescribing provider. Approvals are typically valid for
12 months and must be renewed. Members receiving PA-required medications should confirm renewal
timelines with their provider.


GLP-1 RECEPTOR AGONISTS — DETAILED COVERAGE CRITERIA
------------------------------------------------------
The following GLP-1 medications are covered under Tier 5 (Specialty) and require Prior Authorization:

  - Ozempic (semaglutide injection, 0.5 mg / 1 mg / 2 mg)
  - Wegovy (semaglutide injection, 2.4 mg — weight management indication)
  - Mounjaro (tirzepatide injection — dual GIP/GLP-1 agonist)
  - Victoza (liraglutide injection, 1.2 mg / 1.8 mg)

PA Approval Criteria — Diabetes Indication:
  1. Documented diagnosis of Type 2 diabetes mellitus (ICD-10: E11.x)
  2. HbA1c ≥7.5% at time of PA request, or documented inadequate glycemic control
  3. Trial and inadequate response to metformin at maximally tolerated dose for ≥90 days,
     unless metformin is contraindicated or not tolerated (documentation required)
  4. Prescriber attestation that medication is consistent with the patient's overall
     diabetes management plan

PA Approval Criteria — Weight Management Indication (Wegovy only):
  1. BMI ≥30 kg/m², OR BMI ≥27 kg/m² with at least one obesity-related comorbidity
     (hypertension, dyslipidemia, type 2 diabetes, obstructive sleep apnea, or cardiovascular disease)
  2. Documentation of prior structured weight loss program (≥6 months) with insufficient response
  3. No active eating disorder diagnosis

Initial supply is limited to a 3-month supply. Continued authorization requires documentation of
≥5% body weight reduction (weight management) or meaningful improvement in glycemic parameters
(diabetes) after the initial authorization period.

All GLP-1 medications must be dispensed through a designated Specialty Pharmacy (see below).


STEP THERAPY REQUIREMENTS
--------------------------
Step therapy requires members to try one or more preferred medications before the plan will cover
a higher-cost alternative.

  Diabetes:
    Step 1: Metformin (Tier 1) — must be trialed before insulin analogs or GLP-1 receptor agonists
            are approved, unless contraindicated.
    Step 2: Sulfonylurea or DPP-4 inhibitor (Tier 1/2)
    Step 3: GLP-1 receptor agonist or insulin analog (Tier 4/5, PA required)

  Cardiovascular — Dyslipidemia:
    Step 1: Generic statin (simvastatin, lovastatin, pravastatin — Tier 1) must be trialed before
            brand-name statins or PCSK9 inhibitors are approved.
    Step 2: Atorvastatin or rosuvastatin (Tier 1/2) if lower-intensity statin inadequate
    Step 3: Brand statin or PCSK9 inhibitor (Tier 4/5, PA required)


MAIL-ORDER PHARMACY
-------------------
Members may obtain a 90-day supply of maintenance medications through the plan's designated
mail-order pharmacy at a cost of 2x the 30-day copay (effectively one month free per quarter).

After two fills at a retail pharmacy for a maintenance medication, mail-order dispensing becomes
mandatory. Members who continue to fill maintenance medications at retail after two fills will be
responsible for the full retail cost without plan contribution.

Maintenance medication categories subject to mandatory mail-order include: antihypertensives,
statins, oral diabetes medications, thyroid agents, and long-term psychiatric medications.


SPECIALTY PHARMACY
------------------
All Tier 5 specialty medications must be dispensed through a plan-designated Specialty Pharmacy.
Specialty medications obtained at retail or non-designated pharmacies will not be covered except
in documented emergency situations. The plan's specialty pharmacy can be reached through the
member portal or the number on the back of the member ID card.

Specialty pharmacy services include: clinical pharmacist support, adherence monitoring, copay
assistance coordination, and home delivery with temperature-controlled shipping where required.
